# "What's the Best X for My Car?" — Response Pattern

Source: Training transcript with Dan, 2026-04-08

## Customer Question Patterns
- "What wheels/tires are the best for my car?"
- "What size wheel is best for [car type]?"
- "What's the fastest car you make?"
- "What's the fastest car I can get?"

## Key Rule: Never Answer Directly
These questions **always** require follow-up questions before answering. The answer depends on multiple factors that the customer hasn't provided.

## Dan's Chain of Thought — Tire/Wheel Questions
1. What car do they have? (make, model, specific variant — e.g., "what model of Tyco? They were made from 1970 to 1993, there's 10 different models")
2. What track are they running on?
3. What environment? (hot, cold, etc.)
4. What tires are they currently using?
5. **Then** recommend trying a size bigger or smaller in the same compound

## Dan's Chain of Thought — "Fastest Car" Questions
1. Don't upsell — ask where they are in the hobby first
2. If they're using hobby lobby / toy store cars, a $65 Viper car will blow them away
3. Don't jump to the $400 car if a $200 car would exceed their needs
4. Dan's preferred response: link to the full car chart and say something like:
   > "Based on what you've told me, even our entry-level car is going to be beyond what you're running. It starts at $65, and you can go up from there based on your budget, but you don't need to."
5. Let the customer self-select based on budget — don't push the most expensive option

## Response Template (Tire/Wheel)
> "Great question! To give you the best recommendation, I need to know a few things:
> 1. What car are you running?
> 2. What tires are you currently using?
> 3. What kind of track/surface are you on?
>
> Once I know that, I can point you in the right direction."

## Why This Matters
- There could be dozens of car variants that affect the answer
- Customers may not know the specific model name — they might just say "I have a Tyco"
- Some customers may stop responding when asked follow-ups, but they genuinely need this info to get a useful answer — same as walking into AutoZone and being asked if it's automatic or manual
