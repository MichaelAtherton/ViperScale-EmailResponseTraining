# Product Not Carried — Response Pattern

Source: Discovery call with Dan, 2026-04-06

## Customer Question Pattern
"Where can I find [product that Viper doesn't stock]?"
"Do you carry [OEM/vintage part]?"
"I can't find [product] on your website"

## Response Pattern
- For OEM/vintage parts Viper doesn't carry: clean "we don't carry those" — **do not point to eBay or any other retailer** (rule set by Dan 2026-04-16; see `context/policies.md` → "Never Redirect Customers to Other Retailers")
- For items simply not in catalog: "here's a link to all of our products for that particular car. And if you don't see it, unfortunately, we don't have it"
- If Tyco-related and a Viper Tyco offering fits: provide link to the in-house Tyco offering as the alternative

## Key Examples
- **Magnum 44X2 production wheels** → "we don't carry those" (retired phrasing: "best bet's going to be eBay")
- **OEM/reproduction front wheels** → link to Viper's Tyco offerings if applicable; otherwise "we don't carry those"
- **V3 Magnet Clip** → not carried as a standalone part. See full response pattern below.

## Preferred "Not Carried" Response Shape — set by Dan 2026-04-16

When a customer asks about a specific part we don't stock as a standalone item, the response has three beats:

1. **Acknowledge the customer directly** — "you're right, [specific part they asked about] isn't something we carry at the moment." Name their part back to them so they know we actually read the message. Do NOT lead with something else we *do* carry that they didn't ask for — that reads as tone-deaf or salesy.
2. **State the fact cleanly** — "It's not currently available as a separate part." / "We don't carry those." No hedging, no "let me check," no redirect to eBay or any outside retailer.
3. **Leave a small open door** — something like "Worth checking back though, new parts get added regularly and that could change." This keeps the customer warm without promising anything.

### Gold-standard example (V3 magnet clip reply, Dan-approved 2026-04-16):

> Hey — you're right, a standalone V3 magnet clip isn't something we carry at the moment. It's not currently available as a separate part.
>
> Worth checking back though, new parts get added regularly and that could change.
>
> Keep it in the Slot
> Support Team

### What NOT to do (lessons from the bad first draft):
- Do NOT open with "we do carry the [different version]" — the customer asked for the V3, not the V1. Offering a different version without a confirmed compatibility rule is both tone-deaf AND a guardrail #9 violation.
- Do NOT say "let me check" / "I'll look into it" — email replies go out finished, not as placeholders.
- Do NOT invent cross-version compatibility (e.g. "the V1 fits V3") — only cite compatibility that comes from Dan, the product name/description, or a documented rule in `knowledge/product-rules/`.
