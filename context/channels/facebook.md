# Facebook Response Style — Viper Scale Racing

Source: Discovery call with Dan, 2026-04-06 + to be expanded during /onboard

## Channel Overview
- Dan does **Facebook Lives** — customers comment during and after
- Customers reach out via **Facebook Messenger** (DMs) with product questions and support requests
- Facebook is the **second-highest volume channel** after email
- The Viper Scale Racing Facebook community is active — customers share opinions, builds, and questions

## Tone Differences from Email
- **Shorter** — 1-3 sentences for comments, slightly longer for DMs
- **More casual** — conversational, like talking to a friend at the hobby shop
- **Emoji OK** — sparingly, not every message. Dan's style, not corporate social media style.
- **Still knowledgeable** — Dan's product expertise comes through even in short replies
- **Still direct** — give the answer, don't deflect with "check our website"

## Comment Replies (Public)
- Everyone sees these — keep it clean and helpful
- **Do include:** quick answers, product names, encouragement
- **Don't include:** order numbers, pricing disputes, warranty details, customer personal info
- **Move to DM when:** the question involves order details, payment issues, warranty claims, or anything requiring personal/account info
- **DM redirect phrase:** "Hey [name], shoot us a DM with your order details and we'll get that sorted out for you" (or similar — casual, not corporate)
- **Link format:** full URLs work on Facebook. Include direct product links when relevant.

## DM Responses (Private)
- Closer to email in detail — can include order specifics, SKUs, direct links
- But still more conversational than email
- Can handle the same categories as email: product questions, order issues, warranty, etc.
- Response length: 2-5 sentences typically, longer for complex product questions

## What Dan Avoids on Facebook
- Long-form responses in comments (save it for DMs or email)
- Getting into arguments — if someone gives bad advice in comments, Dan provides the correct info without calling them out
- AI-sounding corporate responses — "Thank you for reaching out! We value your feedback!" — absolutely not
- Discussing pricing disputes publicly

## Facebook-Specific Context
- Dan's Facebook Lives generate a wave of comments and follow-up DMs
- Customers who don't get a quick email response often follow up on Facebook
- The Facebook community includes people with "50 to 70 years worth of opinions" — some give incorrect product advice. Dan wants to be the authoritative voice.
- Some customers are more comfortable on Facebook than email — especially older hobbyists
